Ron (Tyler's Dad) giving a serious talk to Tyler

Close read

  • “The world doesn’t give a shit about you or me. We are not afforded the luxury of being average. We gotta work 10 times as hard just to get anywhere.”
  • No music playing.
  • Body language: Ron is serious and in Tyler’s face, while Tyler avoids eye contact.
  • Framing: the frame is tight and claustrophobic, with very little space between the Tyler and his Dad. This is showing how Tyler feels trapped by expectations.
  • Ron is teaching his son a hard truth about survival living as a black man. But the way he says it feels like pressure instead of love, so Tyler gets suffocated by it.

How it impacted me

  • It reshaped how I see my own parents’ pressure. They weren’t just being hard on me, they wanted the best for me.
  • At the time I felt the pressure, but I now understand where it came from.
  • Like Tyler, I’ve felt the weight of having to work ten times as hard. But unlike Tyler, I use it to push myself forward without wasting anyone’s time.
